Zenburn, I love you.. October, 2011

I use vim. Until recently, I was using a laptop and had a colorscheme called leo on it. Leo offers quite a palette of colours and moderately high contrast.

Switching to a desktop, I had to tweak about with the brightness/contrast settings on the monitors, trying to find the most soothing combinations. Reading code was painful on the eyes. I was still using leo.

I don't recall how I came across zenburn. The effect was pronounced. Zenburn makes your background lighter and uses a more subtle pastel palette. The lower contrast is great for long hours of coding in darker rooms.

What is most surprising about zenburn, however, is that it makes comments look great. The more comments you have, the prettier your code looks. Comments come in a soft green. Very pleasing.

Code I wrote henceforth had substantially more comments.

Simple Interest, very simple
